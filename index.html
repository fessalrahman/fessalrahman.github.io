The Framework

The Agentic Commercial Model

A structured operating model for businesses whose customers — and competitors — are deploying AI agents at scale. Three interconnected problems that existing commercial frameworks were not built to solve.

I

The Consumption Problem

Seat-based pricing assumes a human on the other side of every transaction. Agents don't have seats. The unit of value has changed; the unit of price hasn't.

II

The Broken Comp Plan

Incentive structures built for expansion ARR and seat growth misfire when the customer's buying unit shifts to outcomes and consumption. Sales teams optimise for the wrong thing.

III

NRR Is Lying to You

Net Revenue Retention is a lagging indicator. It tells you where you were. In an agentic economy, by the time NRR signals a problem, the structural shift underneath it is already irreversible.

About the Book

Dead Model Walking

Why Agentic AI Breaks the Business of Software, and the Model That Wins

Every major business is racing to deploy AI. Almost none of them have asked what happens to their commercial model when it works. The pricing models, compensation plans, and revenue metrics that built SaaS were designed for a world where humans were the buyers, the users, and the expanders. AI agents are replacing all three.

Dead Model Walking makes the case that most commercial leaders are not transforming their model — they are relabelling it. Salesforce changed Agentforce pricing three times in eighteen months. Zendesk charges per resolution on top of per-seat fees, not instead of them. The comp plan guidance published in 2026 still has no line item for agent-driven revenue. And the data moat argument at the end is the part most leaders won't say out loud: first-mover advantage in the agentic era is now close to permanent.

About

Fessal Rahman is the originator of the Agentic Commercial Model framework and author of Dead Model Walking — a structured approach to commercial operating model transformation for businesses deploying agentic AI.

He is Group Commercial Director at The Access Group (Hg Capital), leading commercial operations across a portfolio of 20+ software businesses with over $1B in ARR. He is also the founder of FR Advisory, which advises PE- and VC-backed SaaS companies on agentic AI monetisation, pricing architecture, and commercial model transformation.

His career spans McKinsey, Bain, Cloudinary (VP Monetisation), Emarsys, Exasol, and Virgin Media O2 — 20 years of operator experience across enterprise software, strategy consulting, and portfolio commercial leadership.

His thesis: the commercial operating models that built SaaS were not designed for a world where AI agents are the buyer, the user, and the decision-maker. Most businesses haven't noticed yet. The ones that do, and act, will define the next decade of B2B.
